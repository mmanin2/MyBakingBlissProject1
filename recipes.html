Pumpkin Butter Pecan Thumbprint Cookies

  • Prep Time
  • Cook Time
  • Chilling Time
  • 20 min
  • 17 min
  • 30 min
    • Course: Dessert
    • Cuisine: American
    • Keyword: Pumpkin
    • Servings: 24 Cookies

    Ingredients

    • ½ cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
    • ½ cup granulated sugar
    • 1 egg, separated
    • ½ tsp vanilla extract
    • 1 ½ cups all purpose flour
    • ½ tsp pumpkin pie spice
    • ½ tsp salt
    • ½ cup finely chopped pecans
    • ¼ cup pumpkin butter, store bought or homemade

    Instructions

    1. Cream together the butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Add in the egg yolk and vanilla; mix until incorporated
    2. Slowly add in the flour, pumpkin pie spice, and salt and mix until fully incorporated. Dough may look dry. That’s okay
    3. Form the dough into small balls. Press your thumb or pointer finger into each cookie to make a place for the pumpkin butter
    4. Chill for 30 minutes to an hour
    5. Preheat oven to 325 degrees Fahrenheit
    6. Dip the cookies in the reserved egg white and roll into chopped pecans
    7. On a sheet pan with parchment paper or silicone baking mat, place cookies one inch apart
    8. Bake for 15-17 minutes or until cookies are golden and pecans have darkened slightly. Remove from the oven and press down with the bottom of a wooden spoon or spatula to reinforce the well in the cookies
    9. Let cool for a couple minutes, then move to a cooling rack. Fill with pumpkin butter when still warm

    Pumpkin Bars with Maple Cream Cheese Frosting

  • Prep Time
  • Cook Time
  • Chilling Time
  • 10 min
  • 35 min
  • 45 min
    • Course: Dessert
    • Cuisine: American
    • Keyword: Pumpkin, Bars, Cake
    • Servings: 15 Cookies

    Ingredients

    Pumpkin Bars:

    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 2 teaspoons baking powder
    • 1 teaspoon baking soda
    • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
    • 1 teaspoon ground nutmeg
    • ½ teaspoon ground ginger
    • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
    • ½ teaspoon salt
    • 1/2 cup unsalted butter softened to room temperature
    • 1 cup granulated sugar
    • ½ cup packed brown sugar
    • 2 large egg whites
    • 1 large egg
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • 1 can (15 oz) pumpkin puree
    • 1/2 cup plain yogurt or sour cream
    • 1/4 cup milk

    Maple Cream Cheese Frosting

    • 8 ounces cream cheese softened
    • ½ cup unsalted butter softened
    • 3-4 cups powdered sugar sifted
    • ¼ cup heavy cream or milk
    • ½ teaspoon maple extract or ¼ cup maple syrup

    Garnish

    • Pumpkin Pie Spice

    Instructions

    1. Grease and flour a 9x13 inch glass baking dish and preheat the oven to 350 Fahrenheit
    2. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, cloves, and salt. Set aside
    3. In a stand mixer with the paddle attachment or using a hand mixer, mix together the butter and sugars until light and fluffy, about 2 minutes
    4. Add in the egg whites, one at a time, then the whole egg. Then add in the vanilla, pumpkin puree, and yogurt and mix until well combined
    5. Add in some of the dry ingredients, then some of the milk. Continue alternating until all the ingredients have just come together
    6. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and bake for 30-35 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean and the cake springs back when touched
    7. For the frosting, cream the butter and cream cheese in a mixer until light and fluffy. Add in the powdered sugar in batches, then add in the heavy cream and maple syrup and mix together until smooth
    8. Frosted the cooled pumpkin bars as desired. Top with pumpkin spice, if desired. Then cut into bars

    Pumpkin Pie Rugelach

  • Prep Time
  • Cook Time
  • Chilling Time
  • 40 min
  • 25 min
  • 30 min

    • A fun twist on traditional rugelach, this filled spiral pastry like cookie is filled with pumpkin and sweet spices. It's perfect for fall, Thanksgiving and more!

    • Course: Dessert
    • Cuisine: European
    • Keyword: Pumpkin, Cookies, Pie, Rugelach
    • Servings: 20 Pieces

    Ingredients

    Rugelach Dough:

    • 3 cups all purpose flour
    • ¼ cup granulated sugar
    • ½ tsp salt
    • 1 cup unsalted butter 2 sticks very cold and cubed
    • 8 oz cream cheese cold and cubed
    • 1 large egg
    • 2-3 tbsp ice cold water

    Filling:

    • ¾ cup pumpkin puree
    • ⅓ cup light brown sugar
    • 1 1/2 tsp pumpkin pie spice
    • ½ tsp salt
    • Coarse sugar for topping if desired

    Instructions

    1. In a large bowl, combine the flour, sugar and salt
    2. Add in the cubed butter, cream cheese, and egg. Using a pastry blender, cut the butter, cream cheese, and egg into the dry ingredients until a dough begins to form. I like to start with the pastry blender and then go in and use my hands to continue mixing the dough together. If the dough looks too dry, add in 2-3 tsp of cold water (the amount of water you’ll need may depend on the humidity in your kitchen)
    3. Once dough has formed, transfer it out onto a lightly floured work surface
    4. Form the dough into 4 equal circles
    5. Cover each circle of dough with plastic wrap and place in the fridge to chill for at least an hour before assembling
    6. When ready to assemble the rugelach, mix together the filling ingredients: pumpkin puree, light brown sugar, pumpkin pie spice, and salt
    7. On a lightly floured work surface, roll one of the four circle of dough out in a rectangle about ¼ inch thick. Spread the filling mixture onto the dough, leaving a ¼ inch border around all sides. Starting from the bottom of the longer side, roll up the dough into a tight log. Wrap the log in plastic wrap and place in the fridge for 20 minutes to chill before cutting. Repeat this process with the remaining dough. By the time you finish with the final log, the first log should be ready to cut
    8. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit
    9. Take out the first log and slice off each edge with a serrated knife. Slice the log into equal pieces, about 1 inch wide and place onto a cookie sheet lined with parchment paper or a silicone baking mat.
    10. Top with coarse sugar, if desired
    11. Bake for 22-25 minutes or until the pastry is golden brown
    12. Let cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely
